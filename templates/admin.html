How to Interpret These Results
  • Model Comparison Bar Chart: Shows how many transactions each model classified as fraudulent or non-fraudulent for your uploaded data. If most bars are green (non-fraudulent), your data is mostly normal. If a model finds more frauds than others, it may be more sensitive (but could also have more false positives).
  • Individual Model Cards & Pie Charts: Show the breakdown of predictions for each model. A high percentage of green means the model thinks most transactions are safe. A higher red slice means the model flagged more transactions as fraud.
  • Statistical Analysis (Heatmap): Shows summary statistics (mean, std, min, max) for key features in your uploaded data. Helps you see if your data is similar to the training data or has unusual values.
General Conclusions:
  • If all models agree and flag very few transactions as fraudulent, your data is likely safe.
  • If one model flags more frauds, it may be more sensitive or less accurate.
  • Comparing models helps you understand which is more conservative or aggressive in fraud detection.
  • The statistical summary helps you spot outliers or unusual patterns in your data.
